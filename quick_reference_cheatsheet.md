# Prompt Engineering Cheat Sheet

*Print this out and keep it handy!*

---

## The CRAFT Framework

| Letter | Stands For | Question to Ask Yourself |
|--------|-----------|--------------------------|
| **C** | Context | What background does the AI need? |
| **R** | Role | Should the AI play a specific persona? |
| **A** | Action | What exactly do I want it to do? |
| **F** | Format | How should the output look? |
| **T** | Tone | What style or voice should it use? |

---

## The 5 Patterns At a Glance

| Pattern | How to Use It | Magic Phrase |
|---------|--------------|--------------|
| **Zero-Shot** | Just ask directly | *(no special phrase needed)* |
| **Few-Shot** | Give 2-3 examples first, then ask | *"Here are examples... Now do this:"* |
| **Chain-of-Thought** | Ask for step-by-step reasoning | *"Think through this step by step."* |
| **Role** | Give AI a persona | *"You are a [role]. Your audience is [audience]."* |
| **Instruction** | Give explicit rules to follow | *"Follow these rules: Rule 1... Rule 2..."* |

---

## Weak vs. Strong Prompts

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| "Tell me about climate change." | "Explain climate change to a 7th grader in 3 short paragraphs using a simple analogy." |
| "Help with my essay." | "Review my essay intro and suggest 3 specific improvements to make it more engaging." |
| "Summarize this." | "Summarize this article in 5 bullet points, each under 20 words, for a high school student." |
| "Write a story." | "Write a 300-word mystery story for middle schoolers with a surprising twist ending." |

---

## Useful Phrases to Know

**For Format:**
- "as a numbered list"
- "in bullet points"
- "as a table with columns for X and Y"
- "in 3 paragraphs"
- "in under 150 words"

**For Tone:**
- "in simple language"
- "as if explaining to a 6th grader"
- "formally, like a report"
- "in a fun, conversational way"
- "without technical jargon"

**For Reasoning:**
- "think step by step"
- "show your work"
- "explain your reasoning"
- "walk me through it"

**For Refinement:**
- "Make it shorter."
- "Simplify the language."
- "Give me another version."
- "Add a specific example."
- "Make it more [fun/formal/detailed]."

---

## The Iteration Reminder

> **Your first prompt is a draft.**
> Great results come from refining — that's completely normal!

1. Write prompt
2. Read response
3. Refine if needed
4. Repeat until you get what you want
